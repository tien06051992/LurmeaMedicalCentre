Practice Policy

Reminder system

We may give you a reminder phone call or send you a letter if you are due for a follow-up appointment or test. If you do not wish to be part of this system, please inform the receptionist.

Results

Pathology and radiology results are reviewed by our doctors as soon as available and are marked normal or abnormal. When contacting our office for results, it is a practice policy that our receptionists are not authorized to access your results other than to inform you if the comment by your doctor was normal or abnormal. In case that the result was abnormal, a follow up consultation with your doctor to discuss the result is required.

Recalls

If your test results are abnormal then we will send you two reminders. Please ensure you respond to your recall letter to ensure your medical care is optimal. In case of emergency, please ensure that you provide us with your updated address and contact phone numbers.

Referrals

A referral is a formal professional request from a GP for a Specialist service. It must require relevant clinical information regarding the patient’s medical history and the problem requiring the referral. A consultation is required for all referrals. Referrals cannot be backdated.

Contacting your doctors by telephone

To improve patient care, your doctors are not interrupted during consultation except in emergencies. If you have any medical issues that you wish to discuss with your doctor, please do not hesitate to organise an appointment. If the matter is urgent and cannot be delayed then your doctor will be interrupted to provide you appropriate emergency procedures.

Aboriginal and Torres Straight background and other ethnic background

Our practice is accredited for providing care for patients from Aboriginal and Torres Straight background. Please notify your receptionist or doctor if you are from this background.
If you have a specific cultural need, please inform your doctor.

No smoking

We respectfully request that you refrain from smoking at the surgery, this includes the waiting room, the consulting room, the front entrance, and the car park.

Emergency contact

In case of emergency, we may require to contact your next of kin. Please ensure that this is available and updated in our record.

Drugs of addiction

We do not store any drug of addiction on site. We do not prescribe drug of addiction for patients from other practices.

Confidentiality and Feedback

Your medical record is a confidential document. It is the policy of this practice to maintain security of personal health information at all times and to ensure that this information is only available to authorised members of staff.
We will not provide your medical information to any other parties without your written permission. The only exception to this policy is through a court order.
Your feedback is valuable for us to improve our service. If you have any concerns, please contact one of our doctors or our Office Manager.
The government body for handling complaints is NSW Health Care Complaints Commission
